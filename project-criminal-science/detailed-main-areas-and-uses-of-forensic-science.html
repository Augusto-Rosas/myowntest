Detailed Explanation of Main Forensic Science Areas

  1. Crime Scene Investigation: The process of securing, examining, and documenting the crime scene to collect physical evidence for analysis.
  2. DNA Analysis: Techniques used to identify individuals based on their unique genetic makeup, crucial in linking suspects to crime scenes.
  3. Fingerprint Identification: Comparing fingerprint patterns to establish identity or presence at a crime scene.
  4. Forensic Toxicology: Study of poisons, drugs, and other chemicals in the body to determine cause of death or impairment.
  5. Digital Forensics: Recovery and investigation of material found in digital devices like computers and smartphones, often to uncover evidence of cybercrimes.
  6. Ballistics and Firearm Analysis: Examination of firearms, bullets, and gunshot residues to link weapons to crimes.
  7. Forensic Anthropology: Analysis of human skeletal remains to identify victims and determine cause of death.
  8. Document Examination: Authentication and analysis of questioned documents to detect forgeries or alterations.
