Criminal Science (Forensic Science)

As mentioned in the U.S. Department of justice, forensic science is the application of scientific principles and techniques to matters of criminal justice. It encompasses a wide range of disciplines, including biology, chemistry, physics, and engineering, to analyze evidence collected from crime scenes.

Main Areas and Uses of Forensic Science

Here are some of the main areas and uses of forensic science:

  • Crime scene investigation
  • DNA analysis
  • Fingerprint identification
  • Forensic toxicology
  • Digital forensics
  • Ballistics and firearm analysis
  • Forensic anthropology
  • Document examination
